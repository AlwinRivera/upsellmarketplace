Forecasting your revenue with
Upsell Marketplace


Traditionally, it has been extremely difficult for startup companies to forecast sales with any degree of accuracy, and it is easy to see why. With no historical data to indicate any kind of sales volume, you do not really have much to go on, other than whatever market research you conducted beforehand. When you are dealing with upsell subscriptions however, that whole model becomes much more definite, and you’ll have some hard numbers you can work with to forecast your monthly revenue.

Conventional forecasting wisdom calls for including only about 20% of your customer base, because that 20% is responsible for 80% of your business, but that simply is not the case with a subscription-based business. You will know exactly which customers to include in your forecast, because they will be the ones who are subscribing from you currently. You will also know which customers are coming on board this month because their subscriptions will be starting, and their revenues will be added to the volume you already have.

You can probably even predict the number of new subscriptions you will be picking up this month, based on the trend you’ve seen over the past few months. You won’t have to make any adjustments to consider seasonal factors such as holiday sales, because your subscription-based business will forge ahead nicely, regardless of the time of year or the particular season which happens to be in effect. In short, forecasting your revenue will be a much simpler task because you have an upsell subscription business, and because you are working with UM, the experts in the field.

If you have more than one product, program, or digital magazine which you’re offering to consumers, it becomes a simple matter to combine the individual subscriptions for these, to come up with an aggregate which accurately represents your incoming revenue. Thus, forecasting your sales revenue with UM becomes a predictable and repeatable process, which you can rely on for accuracy. That in turn, means that you will be able to arrange for adequate staffing and to make appropriate operational plans that will lead to business growth and more profits.
